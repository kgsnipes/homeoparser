 Nitromuriaticum acidum  
 (aqua regia)  
 * Almost a specific in Oxaluria.  
 * Removes the distressing skin symptoms resembling psoriasis.  
 * Three to five drops three times a day.  
 * So-called bilious conditions; torpid liver, hepatitis and early cirrhosis of liver.  
 * More adapted to hepatic torpor and gastric catarrh common in hot and damp climates and 
aggravated by meat eating and alcohol (Hale).  
 * Constricted anus.  
 * Gravel.  
 Mouth  
 - Gums bleed easily.  
 - Ptyalism.  
 - Constant drooling at night.[Merc.] Cankers; small, superficial ulceration over inside of 
mouth and tongue.  
 - Metallic taste. [Cupr. met.]  
 Stomach  
 - Sour eructations, with empty hungry feeling in stomach; not relieved by eating.  
 - Salivation, worse at night.  
 Stool  
 - Constipated, with ineffectual urging.  
 - Sphincter constricted.  
 - Anus moist and sore.  
 Urine  
 - Cloudy.  
 - Burning in urethra.  
 - Oxaluria.  
 Dose  
 - Five to ten drops, well diluted.
