 Justicia adhatoda  
 (an indian shrub, singhee)  
 * Highly efficacious medicine for acute catarrhal conditions of the respiratory tract (used in 
the beginning)  
 Head  
 - Irritable, sensitive to external impressions; hot, full and heavy head; lachrymation, with 
coryza, profuse, fluent, with constant sneezing; loss of smell and taste; coryza with cough.  
 Throat  
 - Dry, pain during empty swallowing, tenacious mucus.  
 - Mouth dry.  
 Respiratory  
 - Dry cough from sternal region all over chest.  
 - Hoarseness, larynx painful.  
 - Paroxysmal cough, with suffocative obstruction of respiration.  
 - Cough with sneezing.  
 - Severe dyspnoea with cough.  
 - Tightness across chest.  
 - Asthmatic attacks, cannot endure a close, warm room.  
 - Whooping-cough.  
 Relationship  
 - Seems to come between Cepa and Euphrasia, which compare.  
 Dose  
 - Third potency and higher.  
 - Severe aggravation have been noticed from lower potencies.
